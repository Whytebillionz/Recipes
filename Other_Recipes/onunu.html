Onunu Recipe

Discription

Onunu is a tasty mouthwatering delicacy from Rivers State in the south-south part of Nigeria. It is the traditional meal of the Ijaw, Kalabari, Degema and Opobo people of Rivers State and Awara/ASA in Imo state.

Onunu is mashed yam and plantain mixed with palm oil and some salt. it is prepared as a smooth mash (not heavy or glutinous dough like pounded yam). the major type of plantain uesd is over-riped plantain.

Ingredients

  • 10 yam slices

  • 5 fingers of plantain(Preferably over-ripe)

  • A cup of palm oil

  • Salt to taste

Steps for Preparation

  1. Peel, rinse and put yam in an empty pot. Add sufficient amount of water to cover the content of the pot. Rinse the plantain whole and divide into three or four (unpeeled) and cook concurrently with the yam.

  2. Place yam in a mortal(large enough) and pound while hot with a pestle until it is evenly mashed.

  3. Add the plantain haven peeled them (in sufficient quantity) in with the yam and pound both together.

  4. Half-way done, add enough palm oil and salt to taste.

  5. Pound until it is well smooth and your onunu is ready. It can be served with stew or pepper soup full of fish and or meats (Preferably goat-meat for me).
